## Overview

Michelangelo was one of the greatest artists of the Renaissance, excelling in sculpture, painting, and architecture. His works are known for their emotional intensity and technical mastery.

## Early Life

Born in Italy in 1475, Michelangelo showed artistic talent at a young age. He trained under established artists and gained recognition early in his career.

## Major Works

His masterpieces include the statue of David and the Sistine Chapel ceiling. These works demonstrate both artistic skill and deep understanding of human anatomy.

## Architecture

Later in life, Michelangelo contributed to architecture, most notably designing the dome of St. Peter’s Basilica in Rome.

## Legacy

Michelangelo’s influence on art remains immense. His works continue to inspire artists and are central to art history.
