## Overview

Cleopatra VII was the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt. Known for her intelligence and political skill, she played a major role in the power struggles of the Roman Republic. Her life and alliances with Roman leaders made her one of the most famous figures of ancient history.

## Early Life

Cleopatra was born in 69 BC into the Ptolemaic dynasty, a Greek ruling family in Egypt. Unlike many of her predecessors, she learned the Egyptian language and embraced local culture. She became co-ruler with her brother but soon established herself as the dominant leader.

## Political Alliances

Cleopatra formed alliances with Julius Caesar and later Mark Antony. These relationships were both political and personal, aimed at securing Egypt’s independence and power. Her partnership with Antony led to conflict with Octavian, the future Augustus.

## Downfall

After their defeat at the Battle of Actium in 31 BC, Cleopatra and Antony fled to Egypt. Facing inevitable defeat, Cleopatra died in 30 BC, traditionally believed to be by suicide.

## Legacy

Cleopatra’s life has inspired countless works of art and literature. She remains a symbol of power, intelligence, and political strategy.
