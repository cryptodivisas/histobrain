## Overview

The RMS Titanic was a British passenger liner that sank on April 15, 1912, after striking an iceberg during its maiden voyage. The disaster resulted in the loss of over 1,500 lives and remains one of the most well-known tragedies in maritime history.

## Background

Built by the White Star Line, Titanic was designed to be the largest and most luxurious ship of its time. It was considered nearly unsinkable due to its advanced engineering and watertight compartments.

## The Disaster

On its journey from Southampton to New York, Titanic collided with an iceberg in the North Atlantic. The ship’s hull was breached, causing flooding in multiple compartments. Lifeboats were insufficient for all passengers, leading to significant loss of life.

## Impact

The disaster led to major changes in maritime safety regulations, including requirements for enough lifeboats and continuous radio monitoring.

## Legacy

Titanic has become a symbol of human ambition, tragedy, and the limits of technology. Its story continues to be told in books, films, and research.

## Did You Know?

- Titanic had lifeboats for only about half its passengers.  
- The wreck was discovered in 1985.  
- It was on its maiden voyage.  
- It sank in less than three hours.
