## Overview

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most famous landmarks in the world, located in Paris, France. Built for the 1889 World’s Fair, it was initially controversial but later became a global symbol of France. Today, it attracts millions of visitors each year.

## Key Facts
- Location: Paris, France
- Built: 1887–1889
- Height: 324 meters
- Designer: Gustave Eiffel
- Purpose: World’s Fair

## History

The tower was constructed as a temporary exhibit but proved so popular that it was preserved. It was once the tallest structure in the world.

## Architecture

Made of iron, the tower features a lattice design that provides strength and flexibility. Its unique structure allows it to withstand wind and weather.

## Impact / Legacy

The Eiffel Tower is a symbol of innovation and French culture. It remains one of the most visited monuments globally.

## Did You Know?
- It was nearly dismantled after 20 years.
- It grows slightly taller in summer due to heat.
- It was once the tallest man-made structure.
- It is repainted every few years.
