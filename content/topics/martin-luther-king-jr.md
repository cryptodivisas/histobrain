## Overview

Martin Luther King Jr. was a leader of the American civil rights movement. He advocated for nonviolent resistance to achieve equality and justice, becoming one of the most influential figures in modern history.

## Early Life

King was born in Atlanta, Georgia, in 1929. He studied theology and became a Baptist minister, drawing inspiration from Christian teachings and the philosophy of nonviolence.

## Civil Rights Leadership

He led major protests, including the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the March on Washington. His famous “I Have a Dream” speech called for equality and justice.

## Impact

King’s efforts contributed to the passage of civil rights legislation, including the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

## Legacy

He remains a symbol of peace, equality, and social justice.

## Did You Know?

- He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964.  
- His speech is one of the most famous in history.  
- A national holiday honors him in the U.S.  
- He promoted nonviolent protest.
