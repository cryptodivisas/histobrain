## Overview

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located high in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Known for its stunning setting and well-preserved ruins, it is one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world. It showcases the ingenuity of the Inca civilization.

## Key Facts

- Location: Peru  
- Built: c. 1450  
- Civilization: Inca  
- Elevation: ~2,430 meters  
- UNESCO Site: Yes  

## History

Machu Picchu was built during the reign of Emperor Pachacuti. It was later abandoned and remained hidden from the outside world until its rediscovery in 1911.

## Architecture

The site features terraces, temples, and stone structures built without mortar. Its design integrates seamlessly with the surrounding landscape.

## Impact / Legacy

Machu Picchu is a symbol of Incan achievement and cultural heritage. It attracts millions of visitors and continues to be studied by historians.

## Did You Know?

- It was rediscovered by Hiram Bingham in 1911.  
- Stones were cut so precisely they fit without mortar.  
- It was never found by Spanish conquerors.  
- Its purpose is still debated.
