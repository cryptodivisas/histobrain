## Overview

Albert Einstein was one of the most influential scientists in human history. His work transformed physics and changed the way people understand space, time, gravity, and energy. Best known for the theory of relativity, Einstein became not only a scientific giant but also a global symbol of genius and intellectual curiosity.

## Early Life

Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany, in 1879 and grew up in Munich and later in Italy and Switzerland. As a child, he was fascinated by mathematics and by the mysteries of nature, especially light and magnetism. He studied at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich and later worked at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern. This period proved remarkably productive, giving him time to think deeply about theoretical problems.

## Scientific Breakthroughs

In 1905, often called Einstein’s “annus mirabilis” or miracle year, he published groundbreaking papers on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and the relationship between mass and energy. The famous equation E = mc² came from this period. A decade later, he introduced general relativity, a profound new theory explaining gravity as the curvature of spacetime. Einstein’s ideas reshaped astronomy and physics and helped lay the foundation for modern cosmology.

## Legacy

Einstein received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics, specifically for his work on the photoelectric effect rather than relativity. Beyond science, he became known for speaking publicly on war, peace, civil liberties, and Zionism. Although later physics moved beyond some parts of his worldview, Einstein remains one of the most admired thinkers in history. His name has become almost synonymous with genius.

## Did You Know?

- Einstein worked in a patent office before becoming world-famous.  
- His 1905 papers transformed several major areas of physics.  
- He won the Nobel Prize for the photoelectric effect, not relativity.  
- The equation E = mc² is one of the most famous formulas in science.
