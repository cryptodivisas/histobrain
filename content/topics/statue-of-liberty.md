## Overview

The Statue of Liberty is one of the most iconic symbols of freedom in the world. Located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, it was a gift from France to the United States. It represents liberty, democracy, and hope for immigrants arriving in America.

## Key Facts
- Location: New York Harbor, USA
- Dedicated: 1886
- Designer: Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi
- Height: 93 meters (including pedestal)
- Material: Copper

## History

The statue was a gift from France to celebrate American independence and friendship between the two nations. It was assembled in the United States after being shipped in pieces.

## Design

The statue depicts a woman holding a torch and a tablet inscribed with the date of American independence. Its green color comes from the oxidation of copper over time.

## Impact / Legacy

The Statue of Liberty has become a universal symbol of freedom and opportunity. It played a significant role in welcoming immigrants to the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

## Did You Know?
- The statue was originally copper-colored.
- Its full name is “Liberty Enlightening the World.”
- It was a lighthouse for a short time.
- Broken chains lie at her feet, symbolizing freedom.
