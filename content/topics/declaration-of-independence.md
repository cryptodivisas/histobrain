## Overview

The Declaration of Independence is one of the most important documents in American history. Adopted on July 4, 1776, it announced the thirteen colonies’ separation from British rule. It also introduced foundational ideals such as equality and individual rights.

## Key Facts
- Date: July 4, 1776
- Country: United States
- Main author: Thomas Jefferson
- Purpose: Declare independence from Britain
- Location: National Archives, Washington, D.C.

## Background

Tensions between the American colonies and Great Britain had been rising due to taxation and lack of representation. The Continental Congress appointed a committee, including Thomas Jefferson, to draft the document.

## Content

The Declaration outlines grievances against King George III and asserts that all men are created equal with unalienable rights. It emphasizes liberty, life, and the pursuit of happiness.

## Impact / Legacy

The document laid the foundation for the United States and inspired democratic movements worldwide. Its principles continue to influence political thought and human rights.

## Did You Know?
- John Hancock’s signature is the largest on the document.
- It was not signed by all delegates on July 4.
- The original document has faded significantly over time.
- It inspired revolutions in other countries.
