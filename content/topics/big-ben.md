## Overview

Big Ben is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the United Kingdom, located at the Palace of Westminster in London. Although often used to refer to the entire tower, the name actually belongs to the Great Bell inside. It has become a global symbol of British culture and timekeeping.

## Key Facts
- Location: London, England
- Completed: 1859
- Official name: Elizabeth Tower
- Bell weight: ~13.5 tons
- Function: Clock tower

## History

The tower was completed in 1859 as part of the rebuilding of the Palace of Westminster after a fire. The bell, nicknamed “Big Ben,” quickly became famous for its distinctive chime.

## Architecture

The tower is designed in a Gothic Revival style and stands at about 96 meters tall. Its clock mechanism is known for its precision and reliability, making it one of the most accurate large clocks in the world.

## Impact / Legacy

Big Ben is a symbol of the United Kingdom and its parliamentary system. Its chimes are broadcast worldwide, especially during important events like New Year’s celebrations.

## Did You Know?
- Big Ben actually refers to the bell, not the tower.
- The clock is adjusted using old pennies.
- It survived bombing during World War II.
- The tower was renamed Elizabeth Tower in 2012.
