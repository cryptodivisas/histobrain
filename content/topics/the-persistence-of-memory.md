## Overview
The Persistence of Memory is one of Salvador Dalí’s most famous works, known for its melting clocks and dreamlike atmosphere. Painted in 1931, it became a defining image of Surrealism. The piece challenges traditional perceptions of time and reality.

## Key Facts
- Artist: Salvador Dalí
- Year: 1931
- Style: Surrealism
- Medium: Oil on canvas

## Creation
Dalí created the painting during a period of intense exploration of the subconscious. He reportedly drew inspiration from melting cheese.

## Style and Technique
The soft, distorted clocks contrast with a detailed landscape. The imagery reflects dream logic and psychological depth.

## Impact / Legacy
The painting is a global symbol of Surrealism and continues to influence art and culture.

## Did You Know?
- It is relatively small in size
- The background is inspired by Dalí’s homeland
- The figure may be a self-portrait
