## Overview

Malcolm X was a prominent civil rights leader who advocated for Black empowerment and self-determination. His views evolved over time, making him one of the most complex and influential figures of the movement.

## Early Life

Born in 1925, Malcolm X experienced racism and hardship early in life. After time in prison, he became involved with the Nation of Islam and rose as a powerful speaker.

## Activism

He emphasized Black pride, independence, and self-defense. Later in life, after a pilgrimage to Mecca, his views broadened, promoting unity across racial lines.

## Legacy

Malcolm X remains a powerful symbol of resistance and transformation.
