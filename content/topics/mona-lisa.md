## Overview

The Mona Lisa is one of the most famous paintings in history, created by Leonardo da Vinci during the Renaissance. Known for her mysterious smile, the portrait has captivated viewers for centuries. It is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris.

## Key Facts
- Artist: Leonardo da Vinci
- Location: Louvre Museum, Paris
- Medium: Oil on wood panel
- Created: c. 1503–1506
- Subject: Lisa Gherardini (believed)

## Creation

Da Vinci worked on the painting over several years, using subtle techniques like sfumato to create soft transitions between colors. The identity of the subject is widely believed to be Lisa Gherardini, though debates continue.

## Style and Technique

The Mona Lisa is celebrated for its realistic detail and innovative techniques. The use of light and shadow gives the painting depth, while the subject’s expression appears to change depending on the viewer’s perspective.

## Impact / Legacy

The painting has become a global cultural icon, influencing art, media, and popular culture. It is considered priceless and attracts millions of visitors annually.

## Did You Know?
- It was stolen in 1911 and recovered two years later.
- The painting has no clearly visible eyebrows.
- It is protected behind bulletproof glass.
- It is insured for hundreds of millions of dollars.
