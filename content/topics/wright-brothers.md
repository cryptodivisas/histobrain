## Overview

The Wright Brothers, Orville and Wilbur Wright, are credited with inventing and flying the first successful powered airplane. Their achievement in 1903 marked the beginning of modern aviation. Their work transformed global travel and transportation.

## Key Facts
- Names: Orville & Wilbur Wright
- First flight: December 17, 1903
- Location: Kitty Hawk, North Carolina
- Innovation: Powered flight
- Nationality: American

## Background

The brothers were bicycle mechanics who became fascinated with flight. Through experimentation and persistence, they developed key technologies such as controlled flight.

## Achievements

Their aircraft, the Wright Flyer, achieved sustained powered flight. They introduced control systems that made aviation practical.

## Impact / Legacy

Their invention revolutionized transportation, warfare, and global connectivity. Modern aviation traces its origins to their work.

## Did You Know?
- Their first flight lasted only 12 seconds.
- They built their own wind tunnel for testing.
- They were largely self-taught engineers.
- Their work laid the foundation for the aviation industry.
