## Overview
The Moai of Easter Island are monumental stone statues carved by the Rapa Nui people. Scattered across the island, they are believed to represent important ancestors and spiritual guardians.

## Key Facts
- Location: Easter Island (Chile)  
- Built: c. 1250–1500  
- Material: Volcanic tuff  
- Height: Up to 10 meters  
- Culture: Rapa Nui  

## History
The Moai were carved during a period of cultural development and transported across the island to ceremonial platforms.

## Design
The statues feature large heads, elongated noses, and deep-set eyes. Many face inland toward villages.

## Impact / Legacy
They symbolize the cultural identity and ingenuity of the Rapa Nui people.

## Did You Know?
- Some Moai have buried bodies.  
- The largest unfinished one is over 20 meters.  
- They were moved without modern tools.  
- Some wear red stone hats.
