## Overview

Frederick Douglass was a former enslaved man who became one of the most influential abolitionists in American history. Through his writing and speeches, he advocated for the end of slavery and equal rights.

## Early Life

Douglass was born into slavery in Maryland. He taught himself to read and write, which played a crucial role in his eventual escape to freedom.

## Activism

He became a powerful speaker and writer, publishing his autobiography, which exposed the realities of slavery. He worked with abolitionists and advised political leaders.

## Impact

Douglass influenced the abolitionist movement and supported women’s rights and equality.

## Legacy

He remains a symbol of resilience, education, and the fight for justice.
