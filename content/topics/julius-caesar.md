## Overview

Julius Caesar was a pivotal figure in ancient Rome whose military conquests and political reforms reshaped the Roman world. As a general, he expanded Rome’s territories, and as a leader, he centralized power. His assassination marked a turning point in Roman history.

## Key Facts
- Born: 100 BC, Rome
- Died: 44 BC
- Role: General, dictator
- Known for: Gallic Wars, political reforms
- Assassinated: Ides of March

## Early Life

Caesar was born into a patrician family and pursued a career in politics and military leadership. His early alliances and ambitions helped him rise quickly in Roman society.

## Achievements

He conquered Gaul, greatly expanding Rome’s territory. Caesar also implemented reforms such as reorganizing the calendar (Julian calendar) and centralizing government power.

## Impact / Legacy

Caesar’s rise contributed to the fall of the Roman Republic and the emergence of the Roman Empire. His legacy continues in politics, military strategy, and even language.

## Did You Know?
- He was assassinated by a group of senators.
- “Et tu, Brute?” is associated with his death.
- July is named after Julius Caesar.
- He declared himself dictator for life.
