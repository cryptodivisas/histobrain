## Overview

Thomas Edison was one of the most prolific inventors in history, holding over 1,000 patents. His innovations, including the electric light bulb and phonograph, transformed daily life. He played a major role in the development of modern industry.

## Key Facts
- Born: 1847
- Died: 1931
- Nationality: American
- Known for: Light bulb, phonograph
- Patents: Over 1,000

## Early Life

Edison had limited formal education but showed early curiosity and creativity. He began experimenting and inventing at a young age.

## Achievements

He developed practical electric lighting and sound recording devices. Edison also helped create the first industrial research lab.

## Impact / Legacy

Edison’s inventions changed how people lived and worked. His influence is still seen in modern technology and innovation systems.

## Did You Know?
- He was partially deaf.
- He held more patents than most inventors.
- He popularized electric power distribution.
- He worked tirelessly, often sleeping little.
