## Overview

Winston Churchill was a British statesman and leader best known for guiding the United Kingdom during World War II. His speeches and determination inspired a nation during its darkest hours. He remains one of the most influential leaders of the 20th century.

## Key Facts
- Born: 1874
- Died: 1965
- Role: Prime Minister of the UK
- Known for: WWII leadership
- Nobel Prize: Literature (1953)

## Early Life

Churchill was born into an aristocratic family and pursued a career in the military and politics. His early experiences shaped his leadership style and resilience.

## Achievements

He led Britain through World War II, delivering powerful speeches that boosted morale. Churchill also played a key role in shaping post-war Europe.

## Impact / Legacy

Churchill is remembered as a symbol of courage and determination. His leadership helped secure Allied victory and influenced global politics.
