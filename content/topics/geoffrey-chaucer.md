## Overview

Geoffrey Chaucer is often called the father of English literature. His work helped elevate English as a language suitable for serious literary expression.

## Early Life

Chaucer lived in medieval England and worked in royal service, traveling across Europe. These experiences influenced his writing.

## Major Work

His most famous work, *The Canterbury Tales*, features a group of travelers telling stories, offering insight into medieval society.

## Style

Chaucer’s writing combines humor, realism, and social commentary, making his work both entertaining and insightful.

## Legacy

His influence on English literature is profound and lasting.

## Did You Know?

- He wrote in Middle English.  
- His characters represent different social classes.  
- He is buried in Westminster Abbey.  
- His work influenced later writers.
