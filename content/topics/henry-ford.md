## Overview

Henry Ford was an American industrialist who transformed the automobile industry. By introducing assembly line production, he made cars affordable and widely available. His innovations reshaped modern manufacturing.

## Key Facts
- Born: 1863
- Died: 1947
- Company: Ford Motor Company
- Innovation: Assembly line
- Famous model: Model T

## Early Life

Ford grew up on a farm and developed an interest in machinery. He pursued engineering and eventually founded his own automobile company.

## Achievements

He introduced mass production techniques that drastically reduced costs. The Model T became one of the most influential cars in history.

## Impact / Legacy

Ford’s methods revolutionized industry worldwide. His ideas influenced manufacturing, labor practices, and global economies.

## Did You Know?
- The Model T was produced for nearly 20 years.
- Ford paid workers higher wages than competitors.
- He helped popularize the 40-hour workweek.
- His assembly line reduced production time dramatically.
