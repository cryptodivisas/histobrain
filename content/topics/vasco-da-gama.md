## Overview

Vasco da Gama was a Portuguese explorer who became the first European to reach India by sea. His voyage opened a direct maritime trade route between Europe and Asia, significantly changing global commerce and exploration.

## Early Life

Da Gama was born in Portugal around 1460 into a noble family. He received training in navigation and maritime skills, preparing him for a life at sea. Portugal at the time was leading European exploration along the African coast.

## Voyage to India

In 1497, da Gama set sail from Lisbon with a fleet aiming to reach India by navigating around Africa. He successfully rounded the Cape of Good Hope and crossed the Indian Ocean, arriving in Calicut (modern-day Kozhikode) in 1498.

## Impact

This route allowed Portugal to establish a powerful trading network in the Indian Ocean. It reduced reliance on land routes controlled by other empires and marked the beginning of European dominance in global trade.

## Legacy

Vasco da Gama’s voyage reshaped world trade and strengthened Portugal’s position as a major maritime power.
