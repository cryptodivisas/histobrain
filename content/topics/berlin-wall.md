## Overview
The Berlin Wall was a powerful symbol of the Cold War, dividing East and West Berlin for nearly three decades. It represented the political and ideological divide between communism and democracy. Its fall marked a major turning point in world history.

## Key Facts
- Location: Berlin, Germany  
- Built: 1961  
- Fell: 1989  
- Length: ~155 km  
- Purpose: Prevent emigration  

## History
The wall was constructed by East Germany to stop citizens from fleeing to the West. It became heavily guarded and fortified.

## Structure
It consisted of concrete walls, guard towers, and a “death strip.” Security measures made escape extremely dangerous.

## Impact / Legacy
The fall of the Berlin Wall symbolized the end of the Cold War. It led to German reunification and major geopolitical changes.

## Did You Know?
- Over 100 people died trying to cross it.  
- It fell on November 9, 1989.  
- Parts of it still stand today.  
- It became a canvas for street art.
