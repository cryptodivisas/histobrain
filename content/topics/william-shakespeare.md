## Overview

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and one of the most influential figures in world literature. His works, including tragedies, comedies, and historical plays, have been performed and studied for over four centuries.

## Early Life

Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, in 1564. His father was a glove maker and local official, while his mother came from a farming family. Although little is known about his early education, it is believed he attended a grammar school where he studied Latin and classical literature. In his late teens, he married Anne Hathaway, with whom he had three children.

## Career and Major Works

Shakespeare moved to London, where he became involved in the theater scene as an actor and playwright. He was a member of the Lord Chamberlain’s Men, later known as the King’s Men, one of the most successful acting companies of the time. His plays cover a wide range of themes, including love, power, ambition, betrayal, and human nature.

Among his most famous works are tragedies such as *Hamlet*, *Macbeth*, and *Othello*; comedies like *A Midsummer Night’s Dream* and *Twelfth Night*; and historical plays such as *Henry V* and *Richard III*. His writing is known for its rich language, memorable characters, and deep psychological insight.

## Influence and Legacy

Shakespeare’s influence on literature, language, and theater is immense. He introduced thousands of words and phrases into the English language that are still used today. His works have been translated into numerous languages and adapted into films, operas, and modern performances.

Even centuries after his death in 1616, Shakespeare remains central to education and culture worldwide. His exploration of universal human experiences ensures that his work continues to resonate with audiences of all backgrounds.

## Did You Know?

- Shakespeare wrote approximately 39 plays and 154 sonnets.  
- Many common phrases, like “break the ice,” come from his works.  
- He was both an actor and a playwright.  
- His plays are performed more than those of any other writer.
