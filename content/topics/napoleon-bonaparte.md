## Overview

Napoleon Bonaparte was one of the most influential military and political leaders in European history. Rising to power during the French Revolution, he became Emperor of France and led a series of major military campaigns across Europe. His legacy includes both sweeping reforms and controversial conquests.

## Key Facts
- Born: 1769, Corsica
- Died: 1821
- Title: Emperor of France
- Known for: Napoleonic Wars
- Reign: 1804–1814 (and briefly in 1815)

## Early Life

Napoleon was born on the island of Corsica shortly after it became part of France. He trained as a military officer and quickly rose through the ranks during the chaos of the French Revolution.

## Achievements

Napoleon won numerous battles and expanded French influence across Europe. He also introduced reforms such as the Napoleonic Code, which influenced legal systems worldwide.

## Impact / Legacy

Napoleon reshaped Europe politically and militarily. While his empire eventually fell, his influence on law, governance, and military strategy remains significant today.

## Did You Know?
- He was exiled twice, first to Elba and later to Saint Helena.
- He crowned himself emperor in 1804.
- The Napoleonic Code is still used in many countries.
- He escaped from exile and briefly returned to power (Hundred Days).
