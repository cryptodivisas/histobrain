## Overview

Christopher Marlowe was a major figure in Elizabethan theater. His powerful writing and bold themes helped shape English drama and influenced later playwrights, including Shakespeare.

## Early Life

Born in Canterbury, England, Marlowe studied at Cambridge. He became involved in theater and possibly government intelligence work.

## Works

His plays include *Doctor Faustus* and *Tamburlaine*, which explore ambition, power, and human limitations.

## Style

Marlowe’s writing is known for its dramatic intensity and poetic language, particularly his use of blank verse.

## Legacy

Although his life was short, Marlowe’s impact on English literature is significant.

## Did You Know?

- He died under mysterious circumstances.  
- He was a contemporary of Shakespeare.  
- He died at just 29 years old.  
- His work influenced later drama.
