## Overview

Tikal is one of the largest and most powerful cities of the ancient Maya civilization. Located in present-day Guatemala, it is famous for its towering pyramids and temples rising above the jungle. It was a major center of culture, politics, and trade.

## Key Facts

- Location: Guatemala  
- Built: c. 200–900 AD  
- Civilization: Maya  
- Structures: Temples and pyramids  
- UNESCO Site: Yes  

## History

Tikal flourished during the Classic Maya period and became a dominant city-state. It eventually declined and was abandoned, likely due to environmental and political factors.

## Architecture

The city features massive stone temples, plazas, and palaces. Some structures rise over 60 meters, making them among the tallest in the ancient Americas.

## Impact / Legacy

Tikal provides valuable insight into Maya civilization. It remains a major archaeological site and a symbol of ancient achievement.
