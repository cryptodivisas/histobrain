## Overview

Nefertiti was one of the most famous queens of ancient Egypt, known for her beauty and influence. She was the wife of Pharaoh Akhenaten and played a central role in a major religious transformation in Egyptian history.

## Early Life

Little is known about Nefertiti’s early life, including her exact origins. She became queen during the 18th Dynasty and quickly rose to prominence alongside her husband.

## Religious Reforms

Together with Akhenaten, Nefertiti supported the worship of Aten, the sun disk, replacing traditional Egyptian polytheism with a form of monotheism. She was often depicted in art as an equal to the king, participating in religious rituals.

## Representation in Art

Nefertiti is best known today for her iconic bust, which is considered one of the most beautiful works of ancient art. It reflects the artistic style of the Amarna period.

## Legacy

Nefertiti remains a symbol of beauty and power. Her role in religious reform makes her one of the most intriguing figures in Egyptian history.

## Did You Know?

- Her name means “the beautiful one has come.”  
- She was often shown as equal to the pharaoh.  
- Her famous bust is displayed in Berlin.  
- She lived during a period of major religious change.
