## Overview

The Apollo 11 Moon Landing is one of the most significant achievements in human history. On July 20, 1969, astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon, marking a milestone in space exploration.

## Background

The mission was part of the Cold War Space Race between the United States and the Soviet Union. President John F. Kennedy had challenged the nation to land a man on the Moon before the end of the 1960s.

## The Mission

Apollo 11 launched on July 16, 1969. After reaching lunar orbit, Armstrong and Aldrin descended to the surface in the lunar module, while Michael Collins remained in orbit. Armstrong’s first step onto the Moon became one of the most famous moments in history.

## Impact

The mission demonstrated extraordinary technological capability and inspired generations worldwide. It represented a triumph of science, engineering, and human ambition.

## Legacy

Apollo 11 remains a symbol of exploration and innovation, showing what humanity can achieve through determination and collaboration.
