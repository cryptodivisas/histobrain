## Overview
The Great Wall of China is one of the most impressive architectural feats in history. Stretching thousands of miles, it was built to defend Chinese states from invasions. Today, it stands as a symbol of China’s historical strength and ingenuity.

## Key Facts
- Location: Northern China  
- Length: Over 21,000 km  
- Built: Various dynasties  
- Purpose: Defense  
- UNESCO Site: Yes  

## History
Construction began as early as the 7th century BC and continued through multiple dynasties. The most well-known sections were built during the Ming Dynasty.

## Architecture
The wall includes watchtowers, fortresses, and signaling systems. It was built using materials like earth, wood, and stone depending on the region.

## Impact / Legacy
The Great Wall is a symbol of Chinese unity and resilience. It is one of the most visited landmarks in the world.

## Did You Know?
- It is not visible from space with the naked eye.  
- Millions of workers helped build it.  
- It spans mountains, deserts, and plains.  
- It is the longest man-made structure on Earth.
