## Overview

Leonardo da Vinci was one of the most extraordinary minds of the Renaissance. Known for his art, scientific observations, and engineering designs, he embodied the ideal of a “Renaissance man.” His ability to blend creativity with scientific inquiry set him apart as one of history’s greatest thinkers.

## Early Life

Leonardo was born in 1452 in Vinci, Italy. He trained in Florence under the artist Verrocchio, where he developed his artistic skills. His curiosity extended far beyond painting into anatomy, physics, and mechanics.

## Artistic Achievements

Leonardo created some of the most famous paintings in history, including the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. His work is known for realism, attention to detail, and innovative techniques such as sfumato.

## Scientific Work

He filled notebooks with detailed sketches of the human body, machines, and natural phenomena. Many of his ideas, including flying machines, were far ahead of his time.

## Legacy

Leonardo’s influence spans art, science, and engineering. He remains a symbol of human creativity and intellectual curiosity.

## Did You Know?

- He wrote many notes in mirror script.  
- His notebooks contain thousands of sketches.  
- He studied human anatomy through dissections.
