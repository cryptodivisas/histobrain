## Overview
The Motherland Calls is one of the tallest statues in the world, located in Volgograd, Russia. It commemorates the Battle of Stalingrad, one of the most decisive battles of World War II. The statue represents the call to defend the motherland and honors those who sacrificed their lives.

## Key Facts
- Location: Volgograd, Russia  
- Completed: 1967  
- Height: 85 meters  
- Material: Concrete  
- Purpose: War memorial  

## History
The statue was built to honor Soviet soldiers who fought in the Battle of Stalingrad. Construction began in 1959 and was completed in 1967 during the Soviet era.

## Design
The figure depicts a woman holding a sword high while calling citizens to battle. Its dynamic pose and scale create a powerful sense of movement and urgency.

## Impact / Legacy
It remains a powerful symbol of resilience and sacrifice. The monument continues to attract visitors and serves as a reminder of the cost of war.

## Did You Know?
- It was once the tallest statue in the world.  
- The sword has been replaced due to structural concerns.  
- It weighs thousands of tons.  
- It stands on Mamayev Kurgan, a key battle site.
